The early years

I spent six years studying Art in school, recieved a Level Two certificate in Protfolio building in Derry IT and recieved a B1 in my leaving cert exam. During my early years I was constantly drawing and painting! Art ran in the blood as I was surrounded by creativity in my family. I think my artistic flare really began to flourish during Transition Year back in school. During Transition Year, I was involved in a fashion compeition which challenges you to create using everyday trash! Junk Kouture.

This was where I first discovered how creative you can really be, if you put your mind to it! My friend, Chloe and I, decided to make a dress out of a table for the competition which was gas craic!

Art throughout Univeristy

Even though I had built a portfolio and was applying to Art Colleges, I didn't go to study art. I was told by many that there was just no future in it, no way of making any money so, I decided to go to National University of Ireland, Galway instead to study Performing Arts with English and History.
I soon found myself walking into audition rooms and tumbling head first, into the creative world of the wonderful Drama society there known as Dramsoc. Before I knew it, I was saying yes to being involved in any show I could get my hands on be it in costume design, set design, make-up design, directing and even writing and movement direction!
Aswell as all these wonderful opportunites the Dramsoc Committee had so many other roles to try and I was determined to nab the roles of Public Relations. I then went on to be Public Relations Officer on three seperate occasions in NUIG where I learned so many new skills in grahic design, social media management, video editing and essentially, ignited my love for art once again.
